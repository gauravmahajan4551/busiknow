What are the Key Roles of the Marketing & Sales Department?

Small businesses often organize their sales and marketing operations differently than larger businesses, putting more of an emphasis on sales until they have sufficient capital and staff to elevate the marketing function. Understanding what responsibilities a sales and marketing division will need to handle until you more formally separate these areas will help you maximize your resources to boost your sales and profits.

Marketing & Sales Department Responsibilities in Detail

Increase Brand Awareness -

Whether you’re a new company, you’re launching a new product, or you’ve decided to target a new audience, increasing awareness of your brand or products is a good goal to guide your marketing plan.Let’s take the example of deciding to target a new audience. For this objective, success can be measured in the number of impressions, by comparing audience brand awareness before and after the campaign.

Increase Market Share –

This marketing objective is related to the competition analysis discussed at the bottom of this blog. By taking a look at other existing brands in your industry, you can define your specific position in the market, as well as project where you would like to be after your campaign.

Launch a New Product –

Launching a new product presents a unique set of challenges to any marketing department. Informing the public about a brand new product and generating excitement is no small feat. Between developing the communication strategy, pricing, and positioning, this goal can have several different objectives.

Attract New Customers –

Attracting new customers to your brand or products is an important effort for ensuring the relevancy and longevity of your company. Attracting new customers should involve different processes than retaining your current ones.

Retain Current Customers –

On the other side of the coin, increasing customer loyalty is another common marketing objective. It's important to remain invested in your audience and, in fact, some may argue it's more beneficial, since it is always cheaper to keep a current customer than to gain a new one.

Key Positions in an Marketing & Sales Department

Sales and marketing roles overlap in many ways. Traditionally, the marketing department plans the initial product launch, which involves researching target demographics, creating promotional materials and employing other forms of advertising designed to assist salespeople in their efforts.

Marketing manager–

Marketing managers create marketing campaigns, strategies and policies according to variables like customer demand, market trends and organizational goals. They lead their teams in developing new advertising techniques and promotional material. Marketing managers monitor the progress of their efforts and make decisions regarding budget and time constraints.

Market analyst –

Market analysts are responsible for examining data related to marketing and sales efforts. They track the analytics of campaigns, identify market trends and watch competitors' activity closely. Market analysts most commonly work for marketing firms and businesses who employ active marketing strategies, though they may also work as consultants.

Sales manager –

Sales managers oversee a team of sales associates and help them achieve different KPIs (Key Predictive Indicators) and quotas. They're involved in recruiting, hiring and training new employees. Sales managers monitor the progress of their associates and mentor them as needed. They also create sales reports and work to ensure total customer satisfaction.

Product manager –

Product managers ensure a product reaches its full potential based on client specifications. They understand everything about the product, including market research, customer demand and unique value. Product managers lead and direct communication between team members in various departments throughout the development and delivery process.

Retail sales associate–

Retail sales associates perform many roles including stocking shelves, assisting store customers and cashiering. They work in a variety of environments like department, grocery, hardware and electronics stores. They assist in changing the price of products and switching out promotions. Some work primarily in the returns and exchange department.

Social media manager –

Social media managers oversee marketing campaigns on various social media platforms. They create unique content targeted at specific audiences to generate brand awareness and sales. In addition, they communicate with other members of the marketing team to develop new ideas and interact with the company's social media followers. As they implement new content, they monitor the related analytics to determine the effectiveness of the campaign.
